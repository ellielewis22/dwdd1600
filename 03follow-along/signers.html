Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

The Declaration of Independence, signed in 1776, had 56 signers representing the 13 American colonies. Here are some of the most famous and influential signers:

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration and later the third President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Key advocate for independence and later the second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned inventor, diplomat, and one of the most respected Founding Fathers.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress; famous for his large and bold signature.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Influential leader in the American Revolution and key organizer of the Boston Tea Party.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Proposed the resolution for independence to the Continental Congress.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign the Declaration, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Major financier of the American Revolution.

These individuals played significant roles in shaping American independence and the country's early government.
